### Issue: Two RQs Are Overlapping But Need Differentiation

**RQ1**: "In what ways has the media helped create and maintain the rivalry between Angel Reese and Caitlin Clark?"

**RQ2**: "How has the media influenced public perceptions of the Angel Reese and Caitlin Clark rivalry?"

**The Problem:**
- RQ1 asks: How does MEDIA frame the rivalry?
- RQ2 asks: How does MEDIA framing influence PUBLIC perception?

**The Distinction That's Missing:**
- RQ1 can be answered by analyzing media content alone (YouTube titles, captions)
- RQ2 requires analyzing public responses (comments) AND linking them to media framing

**Suggested Refinement:**

**Option A: Keep Both, Clarify Methods**
- RQ1: "How do YouTube video titles and captions frame the Reese-Clark relationship as a rivalry?" (Analyze media content)
- RQ2: "Does media framing of Reese-Clark as rivals predict comment sentiment toward the athletes?" (Analyze comments + test correlation)

**Option B: Choose One Focus**
- Focus on media framing (RQ1): What rival

ry language appears in titles?
- OR focus on public perception (RQ2): How do audiences discuss the athletes?

**Current Approach Treats Them As Same:**
Your operationalization table analyzes: (1) media framing in titles, (2) public sentiment in comments. This suggests you're doing BOTH RQs, which is fine—but methods need to show how you'll CONNECT media framing to public perception.
