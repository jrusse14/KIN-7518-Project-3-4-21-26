### **1. Research Questions \& Significance**



**RQ 1**

**The Question:** How does social media coverage of Angel Reese’s and Caitlin Clark’s public comments—particularly the frequency and framing of their references to one another—contribute to the perception of rivalry between the two athletes?



**The Context:** The rise of social media has significantly changed how athletes are portrayed both by the media and by themselves. As the visibility of the WNBA has grown, two figures have emerged as the most visible athletes: Angel Reese and Caitlin Clark. This is not only due to their on-court success but also because of how their actions and statements are framed and interpreted online. The way the media interprets these actions and statements further perpetuates the personal rivalry between the two, which is typically fueled by the original intent of the athletes being distorted. causing the personal rivalry to become the dominant storyline. This project is informed by media framing theory, which helps explain how selective emphasis and repetition can influence the audience’s interpretation of the content. These frameworks are appropriate because they help to contextualize how coverage and Reese and Clark move from athlete-driven commentary to media-driven narratives.



**Why It Matters:** The perception of rivalries can impact the athletes, fans, and sport organizations. For the athletes, being positioned as rivals can affect public perception and media expectations, sometimes overshadowing team performance and success. From a sport management perspective, rivalry-driven narratives can negatively impact fan engagement and the overall consumption of the sport. Media organizations and leagues have a stake in how these narratives are constructed, as they shape fan culture and the overall perception of the sport.

### 5\. Limitations \& Potential Issues



1. The data sets and software used to analyze the data may not account for spam or repeated comments, which may artificially inflate figures that we receive back after processing the data. This could lead to false conclusions if we are not careful to take into account this possibility.
   
2. Individuals often comment or post with the set intention of causing controversy. The content of the youtube videos as well as the comments could be uploaded with intentionally outlandish opinions to get people to argue. This may change the public perception of the rivalry in an interesting manner that could challenge our approach.
   
3. This “rivalry” did not start until later into both of the athletes’ collegiate careers so there are limitations to the amount of data we have. Additionally, they have competed against each other in different spaces throughout their careers and this itself could change the public perception of the rivalry.

### 6\. Ethical Considerations





**Privacy:** This project analyzes publicly available data drawn from Instagram posts and comments, as well as YouTube comments, all of which originate from public accounts and comment sections. To further reduce privacy risks, the analysis focuses on aggregate patterns and framing trends rather than individual commenters.



**Harm:** There is potential risk in examining both fan commentary and rivalry narratives, as this could unintentionally reinforce stereotypes about female athletes or amplify hostile online discourse.



**Bias:** There is a potential for bias from our group when analyzing the data and addressing the research questions since we attend LSU which is where Angel Reese competed for her college career. It is easy to say that our group will set aside bias, but inherent opinions on the matter may still be present when addressing the question.

### 8\. Data Visualization Plan



**Primary Goal:** For our visualization, we want it to show how fixated (or not) the public is on their rivalry and framing the two athletes against each other. It is important for the visualization to address these comments both currently and throughout the career stages of each athlete.  



**Visualization Description:** A pie chart comparing the percentage of comments that address each variable that the research question focuses on would be beneficial to visualize the public perception of this rivalry. While not every post or comment is angled at the opposing athlete, it will be interesting to visualize what percent of their feeds and comments are taken over by the public pushing the rivalry. For our preliminary pie chart, we used one comparing the proportion of comments that are negative, positive or mixed. We are also using a bar graph to examine the data that outlines the mention percentage based on athlete and platform.



**Design Rationale:** A pie chart is useful in showing parts of a whole and we believe that it will help our argument in showing how much of the space surrounding these athletes is taken up by their public rivalry against each other. Furthermore, it could also show that this is not a public focus if the comments do not take up a majority of their social media activity. We also selected the bar chart because it is a visually simple way to look at data points side by side.
