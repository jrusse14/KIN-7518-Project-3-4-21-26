## 1. Research Questions & Significance

### RQ 1

**The Question:**
What proportion of social media comments about female athletes emphasize athletic performance compared to physical appearance?

**The Context:**
Media representations of athletes play a central role in shaping public perceptions of sport, gender, and athletic legitimacy. Prior research in sport sociology and gender studies has consistently documented that female athletes are more likely than male athletes to be discussed in terms of appearance, femininity, or personal attributes rather than athletic competence (Krieger et al., 2023; University of Cambridge, 2016). Social media platforms amplify these dynamics by enabling large-scale public commentary that is informal, rapid, and largely unfiltered. However, while qualitative studies have highlighted gendered patterns in sport discourse, fewer classroom-accessible projects systematically quantify how frequently performance-focused versus appearance-focused language appears in everyday social media discussions of female athletes. This research question addresses this gap by applying a structured content analysis approach to a large, publicly available dataset of sport-related social media comments.

**Why It Matters:**
Understanding the balance between performance-focused and appearance-focused discourse has implications for athletes, sport organizations, and media consumers. For athletes, persistent emphasis on appearance may undermine perceptions of competence and legitimacy. For sport organizations and media outlets, these patterns inform discussions around gender equity and responsible representation. For researchers and students, this question provides a clear, measurable entry point into examining how social issues such as gender bias manifest in sport-related communication. This question is timely given the growing visibility of women’s sport and the increasing role of social media in shaping sport culture.

## 2. Dataset Selection & Justification

**Dataset Choice:** ☑ Clark-Reese (ARCC)

**Justification:**
The Clark-Reese (ARCC) dataset is well suited to addressing the proposed research question because it contains a large collection of social media posts and comments related to sport and athletes. The dataset includes textual data that allows for systematic identification of language referencing athletic performance and physical appearance. Additionally, the dataset is publicly available and commonly used in instructional contexts, making it appropriate for a course-based research project focused on methodological transparency and ethical data use.

## 5. Limitations & Potential Issues

1. Keyword-based coding may overlook contextual nuances, sarcasm, or implicit meanings in comments.
2. The dataset includes only publicly available social media content and may not represent all audiences or platforms.
3. Coding decisions may be influenced by researcher interpretation, even with clear operational definitions.

---

## 6. Ethical Considerations

**Privacy:**
The data analyzed consist of publicly available social media comments. No attempt will be made to identify or profile individual users, and all analysis will be conducted at an aggregate level.

**Harm:**
There is a risk that analyzing appearance-based commentary could unintentionally reinforce harmful stereotypes. This risk will be mitigated by framing findings within a critical, analytical perspective rather than normalizing such discourse.

**Bias:**
Potential biases may exist in both the dataset and the researchers’ interpretations. The group will address this by using transparent coding rules, discussing disagreements during coding, and acknowledging subjective judgment as a limitation.
