## Overall Assessment

Your plan shows good foundational thinking about athlete image and social media output. You've created a comprehensive set of visualizations demonstrating excellent use of AI tools. However, your research question needs refinement to be more specific and answerable. The current RQ is too broad ("How does social media output affect an athlete's image?") and would benefit from focusing on a more precise aspect of this relationship.

---

## 1. Research Questions & Significance

### Strengths
- Timely topic: The rise of women's basketball provides rich context
- Recognition of brand deal implications (Reese-fashion/beauty vs. Clark-athletic/performance)
- You've identified a real phenomenon worth studying

### Areas for Major Refinement

**Issue 1: RQ Is Too Broad**
- Current RQ: "How does social media output affect an athlete's image?"
- This is essentially asking: "What is the relationship between X and Y?" without specifying what aspect you're examining
- Nearly impossible to answer definitively with your dataset

**Suggested Revisions:**

**Option A: Focus on Content Type**
"How does the type of social media content (basketball vs. non-basketball) posted by Angel Reese and Caitlin Clark relate to audience perceptions of them as athletes vs. influencers?"

**Option B: Focus on Engagement Patterns**
"How do basketball-related vs. lifestyle-related posts differ in engagement levels (comments, likes) for Angel Reese and Caitlin Clark?"

**Option C: Focus on Comment Themes**
"What types of comments (skill-based, appearance-based, comparative) do basketball vs. non-basketball posts generate for Angel Reese and Caitlin Clark?"

**Issue 2: Context-RQ Disconnect**
- Your context discusses brand deals and public image
- But your RQ doesn't ask about brand deals or commercial perception
- If brand deals matter, make that explicit: "How does social media content relate to the types of brand partnerships athletes receive?"

**Issue 3: "Why It Matters" Lacks Specificity**
- "Women's basketball is more popular" is too general
- Strengthen by explaining: How does understanding this relationship help athletes? Marketers? Organizations?

### Next Steps:
- Choose one specific aspect to focus on (content type, engagement, comment themes, or brand alignment)
- Revise RQ to be testable and specific
- Make explicit connection between context and RQ
