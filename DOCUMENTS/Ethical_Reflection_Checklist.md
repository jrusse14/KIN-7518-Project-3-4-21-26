# Ethical Reflection Checklist (KIN 7518)

**Instructions:** Use this checklist for every project to ensure your analysis is responsible and minimizes harm. This must be reviewed before submitting your final report.

## A. Privacy & Consent
- [ ] **Public vs. Private:** Is the data from a truly public forum (e.g., public YouTube comments) or a semi-private space?
- [ ] **Anonymization:** Have we removed direct usernames/handles from our report examples to prevent "piling on" or harassment of private individuals?
- [ ] **Vulnerable Groups:** Does our data include minors or vulnerable populations? If so, have we taken extra care not to expose them?

## B. Bias & Representation
- [ ] **Selection Bias:** Who is missing from this dataset? (e.g., "This dataset only captures English-speaking users with internet access.")
- [ ] **Researcher Bias:** How might our own identities (race, gender, background) influence how we interpret these comments?
- [ ] **Stereotyping:** Does our analysis reinforce harmful stereotypes? (e.g., automatically categorizing AAVE as "toxic" or "aggressive"?)

## C. Harm Reduction
- [ ] **Contextualization:** Do we present extreme comments as "representative" of the whole group, or do we contextualize them as outliers?
- [ ] **Inference Boundaries:** Are we claiming to know what people *think/feel* based only on what they *typed*? (Avoid: "Users felt angry." Better: "Users expressed anger through aggressive language.")
- [ ] **Re-traumatization:** Does re-publishing specific hate speech in our report serve a necessary analytical purpose, or is it gratuitous?

## D. Responsible AI/Tool Use
- [ ] **Tool Transparency:** Do we understand what the provided script/tool is actually measuring? (e.g., knowing that a "sentiment score" is just a dictionary match, not a human reading).
- [ ] **Limitations:** Have we explicitly stated the limitations of our automated analysis in the report?
