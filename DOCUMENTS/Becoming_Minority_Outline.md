# Assignment Prompt: Becoming a Minority (Individual)

**Due Dates:**
*   **Idea Submission:** January 20 (via Google Sheets)
*   **Slides Submission:** Monday before your scheduled presentation date.
*   **Presentations:** February 24 (Round 1), March 17 (Round 2), March 24 (Round 3).

## Overview
This assignment requires you to step out of your comfort zone and experience what it feels like to be a minority in a specific social setting. You will attend an event, place, or gathering where you are an obvious numerical or social minority. The goal is not just to "observe" others, but to **reflect on your own internal experience** of inclusion, exclusion, power, and interaction.

## Instructions
1.  **Choose a Setting:** Select a venue or event where you differ significantly from the majority in terms of race, gender, religion, age, subculture, or ability.
    *   *Examples:* A male student attending a women’s networking event; A domestic student attending an international student cultural night; A non-religious student attending a religious service (or vice versa).
    *   *Note:* The setting must be safe and public. Do not intrude on private, closed, or sensitive gatherings.

2.  **The Experience (Fieldwork):** Attend the event for at least 45-60 minutes.
    *   **Observe:** How is the space arranged? Who talks to whom? Who holds power?
    *   **Interact:** Do not just stand in the corner. Engage (respectfully) if appropriate.
    *   **Reflect:** How did you feel? Anxious? Welcomed? Invisible? Hyper-visible?

3.  **The Presentation (9-10 Minutes):**
    *   **Context:** Briefly describe where you went and why.
    *   **The Experience:** Describe what happened. What were the sensory details?
    *   **Internal Reflection:** How did being a minority affect your behavior, confidence, or comfort?
    *   **Connection to Sport/Society:** Connect this feeling to the experience of minority groups in sport settings. How does this inform your understanding of "inclusion"?

## Grading Criteria (25 Points)
*   **Depth of Reflection:** Moves beyond surface-level description to deep introspection.
*   **Connection to Course Concepts:** Links personal experience to broader sociological ideas (power, othering, inclusion).
*   **Presentation Quality:** Clear, engaging, and within the time limit.
